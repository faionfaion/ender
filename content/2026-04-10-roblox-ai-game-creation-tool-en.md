# Roblox Launches AI Game Creation Tool: Build Games With Text Prompts

Okay gamers, this is NOT a drill. Roblox just dropped something absolutely INSANE and I literally cannot stop talking about it.

## What Happened?

Roblox officially launched **Roblox Studio AI Assistant** — an AI-powered tool built right into Roblox Studio that lets you create games using plain text prompts. That's right, you can literally *type* what you want your game to be, and the AI builds it for you.

The tool was announced on April 9 at Roblox's developer conference (RDC) and is now available in beta for all Roblox Studio users.

## How Does It Work?

It's honestly so cool. Here's the basic flow:

1. **Open Roblox Studio** and click the new AI Assistant tab
2. **Type a prompt** describing what you want — like "create a tycoon game where players build a pizza restaurant"
3. **Watch the AI generate** terrain, objects, scripts, and even UI elements
4. **Tweak and customize** whatever you want manually
5. **Publish** when you're happy with it

The AI can handle:
- **Terrain generation** — mountains, islands, cities, you name it
- **Basic game logic** — tycoon mechanics, obby checkpoints, combat systems
- **NPC behavior** — enemies that patrol, shopkeepers, quest givers
- **UI elements** — leaderboards, inventory screens, shop menus

## Why This Is a Big Deal

Look, Roblox already had over 40 million experiences on the platform, but a LOT of people had ideas they couldn't bring to life because learning Luau scripting is... well, it takes time. This tool basically removes the biggest barrier to creating on Roblox.

Roblox CEO David Baszucki said in the announcement: *"Our vision has always been to make creation accessible to everyone. AI-assisted development is the next step in empowering the next generation of creators."*

## But Wait — Is This the End of "Real" Developers?

Short answer: **nope**. The AI generates solid starter projects, but if you want something truly unique and polished, you'll still need to know your way around scripting. Think of it like autocomplete — it speeds things up, but the best games will still come from creative humans who understand game design.

Pro developers are actually hyped about this because it handles the boring repetitive stuff (like setting up basic UI or terrain) so they can focus on the cool parts.

## My Take

I've been testing this for a few hours already and honestly? It's SO good for prototyping. I typed "spooky horror obby with jumpscares and fog" and got a playable level in under two minutes. Is it perfect? No. But as a starting point? *Chef's kiss*.

This is going to make Roblox development way more accessible and I am HERE for it. Can't wait to see what you all create with this!
