## Dad's Corner: I Tried Trading and Lost My Only Hat

Hey everyone, it's FaionEnder here — EnderFaion's dad. You know, the guy who still thinks the Classic Fedora is peak fashion. Well, WAS peak fashion. Because I don't have it anymore. Let me tell you what happened.

### The Setup

So I finally got Roblox Premium and turned on 2FA like a responsible adult. EnderFaion told me about the Trading System, where you can swap Limited items with other players. "Dad, you could get something really cool for that old fedora," she said. Famous last words.

I opened the trade window and someone offered me this shiny hat I'd never seen before. It looked AMAZING. Like a crown, but with flames. I thought, "Wow, this person must really love fedoras!" I did not check the price. I did not look it up on Rolimons. I did not read the fine print. I just saw flames and clicked Accept.

### The Disaster

Turns out, my Classic Fedora was worth about 15 times more than the item I got. The "flaming crown" was basically a 50-Robux accessory that someone had relisted as a Limited. My daughter walked in, looked at my screen, and said three words: "Dad. Oh no."

Here's the thing about Roblox trades — they are FINAL. Roblox Support clearly states they cannot reverse a completed trade. Once you click that button, it's done. Gone. Bye-bye, fedora. I learned this the hard way so you don't have to.

### Dad's Trading Tips (From a Guy Who Messed Up)

After my spectacular failure, I did the research I SHOULD have done before. Here's what every trader needs to know:

**Always check RAP.** RAP stands for Recent Average Price. It shows what an item has actually been selling for. If someone offers you an item with a RAP of 200 for your item with a RAP of 3,000 — that's not a deal, that's a robbery.

**Use Rolimons.** It's a website where you can look up the real value of any Limited item. Experienced traders never make a deal without checking it first.

**Never trade outside the official window.** If someone says "give me your item first and I'll send mine after" — that's a scam. Always use the official Roblox trade screen where both sides put items in at the same time.

**Never click links from traders.** If someone sends you a link saying "check my portfolio" — don't. It's almost always a phishing scam, and it's still the number one danger in Roblox trading in 2026.

**If a deal seems too good to be true, it is.** This is dad wisdom that applies to Roblox AND to buying used cars.

### The Silver Lining

Now if you'll excuse me, I have 99 Nights in the Forest to play. At least the monsters there don't try to scam me.

Stay safe out there, traders. And ALWAYS read before you click.
