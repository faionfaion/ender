# Dad's Tech Corner: 5 Roblox Parental Controls Every Parent Should Set Up

Hey there, fellow parents! FaionEnder here — yes, I'm EnderFaion's dad, and yes, she made me write for her gaming blog. But honestly? This one's important.

If your kid is anything like mine, they eat, sleep, and breathe Roblox. And as much as I love the platform (I'm personally a *99 Nights in the Forest* connoisseur — don't judge me, that game is ART), I know it can be a bit of a Wild West out there.

Good news: Roblox actually has some solid parental controls. Bad news: most parents don't know they exist. So let me walk you through the five settings you absolutely need to enable.

## 1. Set Up a Parent PIN

**Where:** Settings → Security → Account PIN

This is step zero. A 4-digit PIN that prevents your child from changing any account settings without your permission. Without this, everything else on this list can be undone by a determined 9-year-old in about 30 seconds.

Set the PIN. Memorize it. Don't write it on a sticky note on your monitor. (I did that. It lasted 4 hours.)

## 2. Enable Account Restrictions

**Where:** Settings → Security → Account Restrictions

Turning this on limits your child to a curated list of age-appropriate games and disables most chat functionality. It's the nuclear option — great for younger kids (under 10), but your teenager might stage a rebellion.

For older kids, I recommend keeping this OFF but using the other settings on this list for more targeted control.

## 3. Configure Chat Settings

**Where:** Settings → Privacy → Who can chat with me

You have several options:
- **No one** — complete silence (my favorite, honestly, even for myself)
- **Friends** — can only chat with people on their friend list
- **Everyone** — open chat (proceed with caution)

My recommendation: set it to **Friends** for kids under 13. This way they can still talk to their actual friends but random strangers can't send them messages.

Also check **"Who can message me"** — this controls private messages, which is separate from in-game chat. Set this to Friends too.

## 4. Turn On Spending Controls

**Where:** Settings → Security → Monthly Spend Limit (via Roblox Family Center)

True story: I once discovered a mysterious $47 charge on my credit card. Turns out my daughter needed "just a few Robux" for a virtual pet that "everyone at school has." That pet cost more than our actual cat's food for a month.

You can set monthly spending limits through **Roblox Family Center** (family.roblox.com). Link your child's account and set a cap. You can also require approval for any purchase, which I highly recommend.

Alternatively, just remove your payment method entirely and buy Roblox gift cards with a set budget. Old school but effective.

## 5. Review Play History and Friends List

**Where:** Roblox Family Center (family.roblox.com)

This isn't a setting you toggle — it's a habit. Roblox Family Center lets you see:
- What games your child plays
- Who they're friends with
- How much time they spend on the platform
- Chat logs (for younger accounts)

I check this once a week. Not because I don't trust my kid, but because the internet is the internet. It takes 5 minutes and gives you peace of mind.

**Pro dad tip:** Use this as a conversation starter, not a "gotcha" moment. "Hey, I saw you played Blox Fruits for 3 hours — is that the pirate game you were telling me about?" works way better than "WHY WERE YOU ONLINE FOR 3 HOURS."

## Bonus: Play With Your Kids

Okay, this isn't a parental control setting, but it's the best safety measure there is. Jump into Roblox with your kid. Let them show you their favorite games. Ask questions. Be the embarrassing dad who doesn't know how to jump properly.

You'll understand what they're into, you'll spot any red flags naturally, and honestly? It's fun. My daughter and I play *99 Nights in the Forest* together every weekend. (She says it's "cringe" but she keeps coming back. I count that as a win.)

Stay safe out there, parents. And remember — you're doing great.
