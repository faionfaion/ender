Okay, real talk. I've been waiting for Doors Floor 3 for what feels like FOREVER, and I may have already played it twelve times since launch. So yeah, I have some opinions. Let's get into it.

## The Setup

For those who somehow don't know, Doors by LSPLASH is one of the biggest horror games on Roblox. Floor 1 was iconic, Floor 2 expanded everything, and now Floor 3 promises to be the biggest update yet. The devs teased it for months with cryptic images and ARG puzzles that had the community going absolutely feral.

So does it live up to the insane hype? Mostly... yes!

## What's New

**New Entities** — Floor 3 introduces 5 brand new entities, and they are TERRIFYING. Without spoiling too much, there's one that mimics the appearance of normal doors so you never know if you're opening a safe door or walking into a trap. My heart rate was through the roof.

**Puzzle Mechanics** — This is the biggest change. Floor 3 isn't just "open doors, avoid entities, repeat." There are actual puzzles you need to solve to progress. One involves reading symbols on the walls and matching them in the right order. Another requires you to listen to audio cues in complete darkness. It's so much more engaging than just running and hiding.

**The Atmosphere** — LSPLASH absolutely nailed the atmosphere. Floor 3 has this abandoned hospital vibe that feels genuinely unsettling. The lighting is dynamic — sometimes your flashlight flickers and the shadows shift around you. The sound design is incredible, with distant footsteps and whispers that make you question everything.

**Multiplayer Improvements** — Playing with friends is better than ever. There are sections where you NEED to cooperate — one person holds a lever while another crosses a bridge, that kind of thing. It adds a whole new layer of teamwork (and panic screaming in voice chat).

## What Could Be Better

**Length** — My biggest complaint. Floor 3 is longer than Floor 2, sure, but I finished my first run in about 40 minutes. For something we waited this long for, I was hoping for at least an hour of content. Quality over quantity, I get it, but still.

**Some Entity AI** — Most of the new entities are brilliant, but one of them (I won't say which to avoid spoilers) has kind of predictable patterns. Once you figure it out, it stops being scary. The Floor 1 entities still give me jumpscares because they're more random.

**Performance** — On lower-end devices, Floor 3 can lag a bit during the more visually intense sections. The devs said they're working on optimization, but for now, you might want to lower your graphics settings if your device is older.

## The Ending (No Spoilers!)

I won't spoil it, but the ending is... something. The community is SPLIT on it. Some people love it, some people think it raises more questions than it answers. Personally? I think it's setting up something even bigger, and I am HERE for it.

Let's just say: pay attention to the paintings on the walls. That's all I'll say.

## Verdict

**Rating: 8.5/10**

Doors Floor 3 is a worthy addition to one of Roblox's best games. The new puzzle mechanics are a fantastic evolution, the atmosphere is top-tier, and the new entities will genuinely scare you. The length and minor performance issues hold it back from being perfect, but this is still a must-play for any Roblox horror fan.

If you haven't played Floors 1 and 2, do that first. If you have — what are you waiting for? Go open some doors!
