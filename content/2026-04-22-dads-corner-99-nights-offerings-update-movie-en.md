## Dad's Corner is Back, Kids

Hey gang, FaionEnder here. Faion told me I could take over the site for a minute because — and I quote — "Dad, you won't stop talking about it anyway." She's not wrong.

My favorite game, 99 Nights in the Forest, had the biggest month of its life. And yes, I cleared my schedule. The lawn can wait.

## The Offerings Update: What Changed

On April 11, 2026 at 12:00 PM EST, the devs dropped the Offerings Update and it runs through April 18. The whole flame system got reworked so it feels smoother, and honestly, way more fair for new players.

Here's the cool part: a new NPC called the Sketchy Salesman now wanders up to your campfire at night. He usually shows up around Night 3, but he's random, so keep your eyes peeled. Kids, rule of life from your dad: never trust a guy named "Sketchy." But in this game? Totally trust him. He's got the goods.

## How Offerings Actually Work

There's a pool of 9 offerings you can grab, plus a new Supply Crate system to stash run modifiers and activate them when you need them most.

Pricing is simple. One random offering costs 10 gems, or you can grab 3 offerings at once for 99 Robux. Dad tip: save your gems first. Robux is for emergencies, like when the Cat Entity is chasing your friend through the Jungle Biome at 2 AM.

Offerings give you combat boosts, better resource gathering, and survival perks. Basically, tiny blessings that make your 99-night run way less painful.

## The Stats That Blew My Dad-Brain

I had to read these twice. 99 Nights in the Forest launched March 4, 2025 — it's not even two years old. And it's already racked up:

- 26+ billion plays
- 1.2 billion+ visits
- 14.2 million all-time peak concurrent players
- 7th most-played game in Roblox history

Seven. Out of everything ever made on Roblox. Back in my day we were impressed by a game hitting a thousand players. Now these kids are pulling fourteen MILLION. Wild.

## Wait, There's a MOVIE?!

Okay, I almost dropped my coffee. Deadline announced in April 2026 that 99 Nights in the Forest got optioned as a Hollywood movie. A real film. With a screen and popcorn and everything.

The game is about rescuing four kids trapped in a haunted forest and surviving 99 nights while creatures hunt you. Honestly, that premise is ALREADY a movie. I'm calling it now — this will be the film Faion and I argue about at dinner for months.

## Dad Wisdom, Free of Charge

Here's the thing I love about this game: it rewards patience. You don't win 99 nights by sprinting. You build your shack, then your base, then your little kingdom, one night at a time. Remember that next time homework feels too big. Slow progress is still progress.
