## Hey Gamers, It's FaionEnder — EnderFaion's Dad!

So last weekend I finally decided to try Roblox voice chat. You know, talk to my daughter IN the game instead of yelling across the house. Great plan, right? Spoiler: it did not go the way I imagined.

## The Selfie That Called Me Old

First, you have to do this age verification thing. Every Roblox player needs to pass a Facial Age Estimation check before they can use chat features. You take a quick selfie video, an AI analyzes your face, and it sorts you into one of several age groups.

My daughter got placed in the 13-15 bracket. I got placed in the 21+ bracket. The app basically looked at my face and said "yep, this guy remembers dial-up internet." Thanks, Roblox.

## The Bubble of Shame

When you have voice chat enabled, a speech bubble icon floats above your avatar's head. Everyone can see it. My daughter spotted it immediately: "Dad, why does YOUR character have the talking bubble?" I was basically walking around with a neon sign that said SOMEONE'S DAD IS HERE.

## Spatial Voice Is My Enemy

Roblox voice chat uses spatial audio — other players hear you louder when you are close and quieter when you walk away. Cool feature! Unless you are a dad who just walked past a group of players while saying, "Honey, did you feed the cat before we started playing?"

I could hear them laughing. The voice got quieter as I ran away, but the embarrassment did not.

## The 99 Nights Incident

Naturally I had to test voice chat in my favorite game, 99 Nights in the Forest. It is a spooky survival game with some super intense jump scares. Now imagine a big server full of players, and one of them is a grown man screaming "WHAT WAS THAT" every four minutes.

That man was me. The whole server heard it. And my daughter? She used the mute button on me. Apparently you can click the little speaker icon above someone's avatar and mute them just like that. She figured it out in three seconds. I spent 20 minutes talking to myself before I realized nobody could hear me. Classic dad moment.

## Quick Voice Chat Facts for Parents

If you are a parent thinking about trying this, here is what you need to know. Kids under 13 cannot use voice chat at all. Players aged 13 to 15 can only voice chat with trusted friends. Players 16 and older get full access. Roblox monitors all voice communication with AI and auto-mutes inappropriate language. You can manage everything through Parental Controls.

The safety setup is solid. The only thing it cannot protect your kids from is their dad making weird noises during jump scares.

## Dad's Verdict

Voice chat on Roblox is actually pretty awesome. It makes games feel way more alive. Just maybe warn your squad before your parents join the server.

And to my daughter — I am sorry about the screaming. But that shadow creature came out of NOWHERE.

Stay safe out there, gamers. And remember, your parents are only embarrassing because we love you.
