## Roblox Plus — A Brand-New Subscription

Say hello to Roblox Plus! Launching globally on April 30, this new $4.99/month subscription is replacing Roblox Premium. If you already have Premium, don't worry — yours stays active. But new players will sign up for Plus instead.

So what do you get? A 10% discount on avatar items and in-game stuff right away, and if you stay subscribed for 3 months straight, that bumps up to 20%. You also get free unlimited private servers, fee-free Robux transfers to your friends, and the ability to trade and resell avatar items. Plus, you get a shiny Plus logo on your profile. Flex!

Oh, and creators — your payouts stay the same. Roblox covers the discounts out of their own pocket. W move.

## PS5 Goes Native

PS5 players, your time has come! On April 14, Roblox dropped a fully native PlayStation 5 app. Before this, PS5 was just running the old PS4 version, which was... fine. But now? Up to 30% faster load times, smoother gameplay, and — this is the cool part — DualSense haptic feedback support.

That means developers can make you actually feel stuff through your controller. Imagine feeling every footstep in your favorite obby or the rumble of a car engine in a racing game. Studio Voldex, the team behind NFL Universe Football, already confirmed they can push way more content and better visuals now. GG, PlayStation squad.

## Roblox Kids and Roblox Select

Roblox also announced two new account types rolling out in early June. Roblox Kids is for players ages 5 to 8 — it only shows games rated Minimal or Mild, and all chat is turned off by default. Roblox Select is for ages 9 to 15 and gives access to thousands of curated games rated up to Moderate.

The cool thing? Your account automatically upgrades as you grow up. Kids moves to Select at age 9, and Select becomes a regular Roblox account at 16. Parents also get expanded controls to block specific games, manage chat, and set screen-time and spending limits.

## Quick Bonus Updates

Roblox quietly renamed "Connections" back to just "Friends" on April 7. The default death animation in new games is now a ragdoll instead of the classic break-apart. And avatar makeup hit the Marketplace at the end of March. Glow up season is real!
