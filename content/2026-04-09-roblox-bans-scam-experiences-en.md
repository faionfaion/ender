## Roblox just went full cleanup mode

Hey everyone, EnderFaion here! 👋

So, HUGE news just dropped — Roblox has officially banned **over 10,000 scam experiences** from the platform in what they're calling their biggest safety sweep of 2026. And honestly? It's about time.

## What happened?

Roblox's Trust & Safety team announced on April 7 that they completed a massive review of reported experiences. The result: **10,247 games were permanently removed** for violating community guidelines, specifically for:

- **Fake "Free Robux" generators** — you know, those games that promise you a million Robux if you just enter your password? Yeah, those. Gone.
- **Phishing experiences** — games designed to steal your account info by mimicking the Roblox login screen.
- **Misleading thumbnails and titles** — experiences that pretend to be popular games like Blox Fruits or Adopt Me but are actually just scam traps.
- **Bot-driven fake engagement** — games using automated players to look popular and trick real users into joining.

## How to spot a scam experience

Look, I know some of these scams can look pretty convincing, so here's a quick checklist:

1. **No game can give you free Robux.** Period. If it says it can, it's a scam.
2. **Never enter your password inside a game.** Roblox will NEVER ask for it in an experience.
3. **Check the developer profile.** New accounts with no other games? Red flag.
4. **Read the reviews.** If people are warning about scams in the comments, listen to them.
5. **If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is.** Classic rule, works every time.

## What Roblox is doing next

According to their blog post, Roblox is rolling out **enhanced AI moderation** that can detect scam patterns before experiences even go live. They're also adding a new "Verified Experience" badge for games that pass extra security checks.

This is a big W for the community. Stay safe out there, gamers! 💪
