## Obbys Are STILL Taking Over Roblox

Okay besties, can we talk about how obbys literally never get old?! It's 2026 and obstacle courses are STILL one of the most popular genres on all of Roblox. Tower of Hell alone has over 20 billion visits — and that's just ONE game! If you haven't tried one yet, you are seriously missing out and today's the perfect day to start!

Whether you're a total noob or a hardcore parkour legend, I've got the full rundown on the hottest obbys, the sneakiest pro tips, and even how to build your own from scratch. Let's gooo!

## The Obbys You NEED to Play Right Now

**Tower of Hell** is still the GOAT. Every round gives you a randomly generated tower with zero checkpoints, and you only have 8 minutes to reach the top. No two games are ever the same, which means you can never get bored. It's pure skill, pure chaos, pure fun.

**Mega Fun Obby** has thousands of levels — and they keep adding more all the time. Each one has a different theme and color scheme, so you could play this one for weeks and still not finish.

And if you want something totally unhinged, try **Troll Obby** — the obstacles literally trick you. Nothing is what it seems. Also shoutout to **Escape McDonalds Obby** (a fan-made game, not official McDonald's!) because jumping over giant french fries is peak comedy.

## Pro Tips That Actually Work

Here's the biggest secret in obbys: **stop rushing**. I know it feels like you need to go fast, but slowing down and focusing on where you land will save you SO many falls. Trust me on this one.

Another huge tip — **you don't always have to jump**. On logs, thin platforms, and checker-style sections, just walking across is way safer and actually faster. Your brain says jump, but your brain is lying to you.

For invisible walk sections, watch the little green circle under your character. That circle shows exactly where you're headed. Follow it and you'll stop falling into the void.

And honestly? **Watch a walkthrough video** before you attempt the hardest levels. There's no shame in scouting ahead. Even speedrunners study the course first.

## Tower of Hell Bonus Secrets

Tower of Hell has some cool server-wide effects that pop up during rounds. Sometimes the whole server gets bonuses like extra time or special abilities — when that happens, take advantage and push as far as you can!

Also, be careful with mutators. Invincibility is amazing — zero downsides. But high speed and low gravity sound cool until you hit a headhitter section and literally cannot pass it. Choose wisely!

## Want to Build Your Own Obby? Here's How!

This is where it gets REALLY fun. Open up **Roblox Studio** and pick the **Obby template** to get started instantly. It gives you a basic layout with a spawn point and a few platforms so you're not starting from zero.

Next, search the **Toolbox** for "Obby Kit," "Reset Part" (parts that reset your character when touched), and "Checkpoint" to grab ready-made assets. Drag them into your workspace and arrange them however you like. Start simple — a few jumps, a moving platform, maybe a spinning obstacle — and then build up from there.

Here are some tips to make your obby actually stand out:

- **Theme your sections.** A lava world, an ice cave, a candy kingdom — themed areas make players want to keep going to see what's next.
- **Use particle effects and bright colors.** Sparkles, fire effects, and neon materials make everything look 10x cooler.
- **Add difficulty progression.** Start easy so beginners don't rage-quit on stage one, then ramp up the challenge.
- **Place checkpoints generously.** Nobody likes replaying 20 stages because they missed one jump. Be kind to your players!
- **Test EVERYTHING before you publish.** Play through your entire obby start to finish. Broken jumps, glitchy teleporters, or impossible gaps will make players leave instantly.

Oh, and don't forget to make a super eye-catching **thumbnail** with bold text and a cool screenshot of your best section. That's how you get those first clicks!

Once you're happy with it, hit **Publish to Roblox** and share the link with your friends. Who knows — your obby might be the next big thing!
