Hello, internet. It's FaionEnder here — also known as "EnderFaion's dad" or "that guy who keeps dying in Doors." My daughter has been bugging me to write something for her site, so here I am. A 40-something-year-old man reviewing Roblox games. My teenage self would be so confused.

Look, when my daughter first asked me to play Roblox with her, I thought it was going to be painful. I was a "real games" person — you know, the type who thinks anything without realistic graphics isn't worth playing. But after a few sessions... I have to admit, some of these games are genuinely good. Like, embarrassingly good.

So here are 5 Roblox games that I, a grown adult human, actually enjoy. Don't tell my coworkers.

## #5: Natural Disaster Survival

This game is basically "stand on a structure and try not to die while nature destroys everything around you." Tornadoes, floods, earthquakes, meteors — you name it. It's chaos and I love it.

**Why I like it:** It reminds me of those old Flash games I used to play at work (don't tell my boss). It's simple, rounds are quick, and watching my daughter panic when a tornado picks up our entire building is comedy gold.

**Dad rating:** 7/10 — Simple fun, perfect for a quick session before dinner.

## #4: Build a Boat for Treasure

You build a boat out of random materials and then sail it through an obstacle course to reach treasure. Sounds simple? It is. That's why it's great.

**Why I like it:** I'm an engineer at heart, and this game actually rewards creative building. My daughter makes beautiful boats with decorations. I make ugly boxes that somehow work better. She's not happy about it and I find that hilarious.

**Dad joke moment:** I told her my boat design philosophy is "if it floats, it's a yacht." She didn't laugh. Nobody ever laughs.

**Dad rating:** 8/10 — Actually engaging engineering puzzles disguised as a kids' game.

## #3: Blox Fruits

Okay, this one I was NOT expecting to like. It's basically an anime-inspired fighting game with fruit powers (think One Piece). My daughter had to explain the entire power system to me three times before I understood anything.

**Why I like it:** The combat is surprisingly satisfying. Once you learn the combos, there's real depth here. Also, I finally found a fruit power that lets me fly around the map, and now I feel like a superhero. I may have played this one without my daughter a few times. Don't judge me.

**Dad rating:** 8/10 — Deep, addictive, but the learning curve nearly made me quit.

## #2: Doors

Yes, the horror game. Yes, I'm a grown man who screams at a Roblox game. We've established this.

**Why I like it:** Doors is genuinely well-designed horror. The entity mechanics are clever, the atmosphere is creepy without being too much for kids, and playing it together is an absolute bonding experience. There's nothing quite like grabbing your daughter's virtual character and running for your lives while screaming "HIDE HIDE HIDE" in voice chat.

My daughter says I'm the "worst Doors player she's ever seen." I say I'm just providing moral support from behind the closet.

**Dad rating:** 9/10 — Best co-op horror experience on Roblox. Yes, I've played Floor 3. Yes, I died. A lot.

## #1: 99 Nights in the Forest

And here it is. My number one. The game that made me a Roblox convert.

For those who don't know, 99 Nights in the Forest is a survival game where you have to last 99 in-game nights in a spooky forest. You build shelter, gather resources, and deal with increasingly weird creatures. It's like Minecraft meets Don't Starve meets my camping trips from the 90s.

**Why I love it:** This game speaks to the outdoorsy dad in me. The survival mechanics are thoughtful — you actually need to manage your resources, plan your shelter layout, and learn creature patterns. But it also has this cozy campfire vibe that makes me nostalgic for actual camping (minus the bugs and the back pain).

My daughter and I have a running tradition: every Friday evening, we do a full 99-night run together. She handles combat (she's way better at it), I handle base building (my engineering skills finally paying off). We've completed it 7 times now and I still find new strategies each run.

**Dad joke moment:** I told my daughter this game is like real camping — except in real camping, the scariest thing is the portable toilet. She told me to stop. I won't.

**Dad rating:** 10/10 — This is the game that made me understand why my kid loves Roblox. Genuinely great game design.

## Final Dad Thoughts

If you're a parent and your kid plays Roblox, try playing WITH them. Yes, you'll be confused at first. Yes, you'll die a lot. Yes, they'll make fun of you. But honestly? Some of my favorite memories with my daughter are from playing these games together.

Also, I'm now the second-best 99 Nights player in our household. Progress!
