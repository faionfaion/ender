## Hey Kids, It's FaionEnder Here

So last night I was trying to play 99 Nights in the Forest, and my game was lagging like a snail riding a turtle through peanut butter. I figured, "How hard can it be to fix FPS?" Famous last words, am I right?

## Step One: Close Chrome

First I noticed I had Chrome open with — and I'm not proud of this — 47 tabs. Forty-seven! Half of them were recipes I'll never cook. Turns out Chrome with that many tabs eats up 2 to 4 GB of RAM all by itself. I closed them and boom, instant improvement. Dad tip number one: close your browser before gaming. Your computer will thank you.

## Step Two: The Graphics Slider

Next I found the Graphics Quality slider in Roblox settings. It goes from 1 to 10, and mine was sitting at 10 like some kind of fancy person. I cranked it down to 3 and my FPS nearly tripled. Sure, the shadows disappeared and the trees looked like they were drawn by me in art class, but the game was SMOOTH. Going from Quality 10 to Quality 1-3 can give you 2 to 3 times more FPS. Just switch Graphics Mode from Auto to Manual first.

## Step Three: Where I Messed Up

Then I got cocky. I read online that you should make sure Roblox uses your dedicated GPU instead of the built-in one. So I went into Windows Settings, found Display, then Graphics, and started clicking things. Long story short, I accidentally reset all my Roblox custom settings. My keybinds? Gone. My camera sensitivity? Default. EnderFaion walked in and just sighed.

## The Tips That Actually Work

After my daughter stopped laughing at me, she reminded me about stuff that ACTUALLY helps without breaking things:

- Turn on Game Mode in Windows Settings under Gaming
- Use a wired internet cable instead of Wi-Fi for less lag
- Update your GPU drivers from NVIDIA or AMD
- Close Discord overlay and recording apps before playing
- Use Fullscreen mode for better performance

Oh, and here's a big one — Roblox now has a built-in FPS unlocker! Since May 2024, you can set your max FPS to 60, 120, 144, or even 240 right in the settings. No sketchy third-party tools needed.

## Dad's Final Wisdom

Sometimes the best fix is the simplest one. Close your tabs, lower your graphics, and for the love of all things blocky, don't randomly click settings you don't understand. Trust me on that one.

Now if you'll excuse me, I need to redo all my keybinds. Again.
