## 25 Billion Plays and Counting

Okay besties, let's talk about the OG detective game that literally EVERYONE on Roblox has played at least once. Murder Mystery 2 by Nikilis just hit 25 billion visits, and honestly? That number is absolutely insane. This game has been around since 2014 — that's over 12 years! — and it still pulls around 188,000 players at the same time.

So is MM2 still worth your time in 2026? Let's break it down!

## The Classic Formula Still Slaps

If you somehow haven't tried it yet, here's the deal. Every round, one player becomes the Murderer with a knife, one gets the Sheriff role with a gun, and everyone else is an Innocent just trying to survive. It sounds simple, but the social chaos is what makes it legendary. Figuring out who the murderer is through chat and watching everyone act sus? Chef's kiss.

Rounds are quick, so you're never stuck waiting long. You earn XP every match, and winning special modes can net you 50 to 100 XP per victory.

## What's New This Year?

Nikilis dropped a Valentine's Update back in February that ran until March 16, bringing exclusive seasonal knives and effects. The seasonal events — Halloween, Christmas, Valentine's — are still the biggest content drops, and the community goes absolutely feral for new collectibles every time.

One heads-up though: there are zero active codes right now. Nikilis hasn't released new ones in a while, so don't fall for any fake code videos!

## The Trading Scene Is Wild

MM2's knife trading economy is honestly one of the deepest on all of Roblox. The Corrupt Knife is still the ultimate flex — traders want multiple Chromas just for a shot at it. The Traveler's Gun sits at the top of Godly values around 4,100, and the Evergun is the breakout star of 2026, surging up to nearly 2,900 in value.

If you're into trading, stick to trusted sites like MM2Values or Traderie to check prices. And please, PLEASE watch out for scammers. Always double-check trades before accepting!

## The Verdict

Murder Mystery 2 is like that favorite hoodie you keep wearing — it's comfy, reliable, and somehow never gets old. Sure, newer games like Twisted Murderer and Dead Silence offer cool twists, but nothing quite matches MM2's vibe and massive community. With 20.7 million favorites and a trading scene that keeps players hooked for years, this classic still absolutely deserves a spot on your favorites list.
