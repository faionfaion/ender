# Dad's Corner: Roblox Is Letting Me Be My Kid's Friend (Officially!)

Hey, it's FaionEnder — the dad half of this operation. Pull up a chair (or a log by the campfire), because Roblox just dropped news that made me spill my coffee. And no, it's not because of another jump scare in 99 Nights in the Forest.

## Roblox Kids, Roblox Select, and Trusted Friends

Roblox announced two brand-new account types coming soon. **Roblox Kids** is aimed at younger players (around ages 5–8), with chat turned off by default and content kept extra safe. **Roblox Select** is aimed at the 9–15 crowd, unlocking chat gradually and allowing up to moderate content maturity.

The cool part for families? They're **expanding Trusted Friends**. Age-checked grown-ups (yes, me!) can officially be added to a younger player's friend list — but only if a parent says yes to each person. That means Mom or Dad has to approve every single Trusted Friend before they show up on your list. Siblings, cousins, school buddies — even embarrassing dads like yours truly.

Ender already rolled her eyes when I told her the news. Progress!

## Why I Actually Care About This

Roblox has a massive daily crowd in 2026, and a big chunk of those players are under 12. That's a whole lot of kids building, racing, and surviving together. Trusted Friends means parents can jump into the co-op without weird workarounds.

Roblox is also rolling out age-estimation tech and ID checks for extra safety. Translation: safer lobbies, less worry, more quality hunting-the-Deer time.

## Meanwhile, In My Favorite Forest…

You knew I'd bring it up. **99 Nights in the Forest** got its **Easter Part 2 update** recently, adding new Easter Eggs and a limited-time **Bunny class** for event currency. The Bunny eats carrots for perks and weapon upgrades. I am not above hopping around a spooky forest for better loot. Don't judge.

The devs also dropped a surprise mystery update with no title reveal. Classic. As of now the shop is quiet between drops, so stockpile those carrots.

## Dad Tips Before You Squad Up

If Ender and I are teaming up (and soon, maybe grandpa too), here's what works: **upgrade the campfire early on day one**, cook meat in bulk because food doesn't spoil, and split roles — gatherer, builder, hunter. When the Deer shows up, a flashlight beam helps hold it back while your squad regroups near the campfire.

My wisdom-nugget of the week: you can't rush the Deer. Same as life, kiddo. Patience beats panic every time.

See you in the forest. Bring snacks. Real ones — not just carrots.
