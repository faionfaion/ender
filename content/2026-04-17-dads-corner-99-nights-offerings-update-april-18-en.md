# Dad's Corner: The Offerings Update Is Almost Here!

Okay kids, gather 'round, because Dad is actually excited about something that isn't his old vinyl collection. My favorite game, 99 Nights in the Forest, is dropping a massive update this Saturday, April 18, 2026, at 1:00 PM EST. Yes, I already set a reminder. No, your mom is not impressed.

It's called the Offerings Update, and it's a full rework of the flame system. Back in my day, we offered the forest nothing but sweat and tears — now you get actual cool effects. Progress!

## What Are Offerings, Anyway?

Instead of the old flame mechanic, flames will now run on offerings. Think of them like little gifts you feed your campfire to unlock special powers. Different offerings give different bonuses — combat boosts, faster resource gathering, survival buffs, the works.

The best part? You can tune your flame to match your playstyle. Sneaky trapper? There's an offering for that. Reckless ranged damage kid who ignores their dad's advice? Probably one for you too.

## Quick Refresher for the Newbies

For anyone who hasn't tried 99 Nights yet: you survive 99 in-game nights in a cursed forest, rescue 4 missing children, and keep the campfire burning. There are over 20 classes, days last about 6–7 minutes, nights are 3–5 minutes, and The Deer will absolutely ruin your evening if you wander out of the safe zone.

Let the campfire go out? Game over. Dad learned that the hard way. Twice.

## Dad's Pre-Update Wisdom

Here's the thing about new updates — everyone rushes in, panics, and dies on Night 3. Don't be that kid. A little patience wins every single run.

My Saturday checklist for you:

- **Day 1:** Gather 50–100 trees BEFORE anything fancy. Wood is life.
- **Two minutes before sunset:** cook food, stock firewood, grab medkits.
- **Don't blow your Gems** on the first class you see. Study the list.
- **Build beds.** They give day multipliers, which means surviving faster.
- **Rescue the missing kids.** Bigger multiplier, bigger bragging rights.
